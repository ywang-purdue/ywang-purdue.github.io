About Me

I am a Graduate Research Assistant in the Video and Image Processing Laboratory (VIPER) in the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) at Purdue University, under the direction of Professor Edward J. Delp. I am also a Ph.D. Candidate in ECE at Purdue University. I received my B.A from Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics in 2012.

Research

The VIPER Lab, directed by Professor Edward J. Delp, is equipped with state-of-the-art technology to digitize, store, process, stream, and display digital images and video. VIPER supports research in areas such as the development of new image analysis techniques, new image and video compression methods, the development of new surveillance and detection methods, new media and wireless streaming technologies, and creating multimedia material for use in media indexing, storage, and retrieval.

I have been working on the Technology Assistant Dietary Assessment (TADA) project since I joined VIPER. I'm the chief Android developer and associate iOS developer for the TADA mobile applications. My research interest lies in image processing (segmentation, quality enhancement, etc.), pattern recognition and deep learning.
